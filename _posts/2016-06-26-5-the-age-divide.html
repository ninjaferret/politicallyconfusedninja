--- layout: default title: The EU Referendum - The age divide summary: So, the UK has voted to leave the EU, but why is there such a divide, on turnout and opinion, between the young and the old? categories: - EU Referendum ---

The older the age group in the EU Referendum, the more likely they were to vote for Leave, and the more likely they were to vote. This means that the older age groups had a disproportionate impact on the EU Referendum result. Many younger people are angry at the older generations, for damaging their future, but many other questions need to be asked. Why do the younger voters not turn out? Why are the older voters more likely to turn out? Why did the generations vote so differently?
